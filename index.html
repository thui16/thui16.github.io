About Me

Hello! My name is Tiffani Hui and I am a third-year PhD student in the Tufts Chemistry Department. I am advised by Dr. Yu-Shan Lin, and we are a computational chemistry group interested in using computational chemistry techniques to study biomolecules, particularly cyclic peptides. My research interests are at the intersection of machine learning and computational chemistry. Prior to graduate school, I had recieved a B.A. in Biochemistry at Wellesley College and have had multiple academic and industry research experiences. I'm grateful to have had opportunities in both fields and witness first-hand the drug-development process, along with the fundamental research that can be applied to/support the pharmaceutical industry. Also having a deeper appreciation for the time-consuming process of drug-development has fueled my passion in being able to develop methods that will aid in rational drug design and structure-based design.
